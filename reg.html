Our Dream Japan Honeymoon Itinerary


Toyko

Highlights: Ghibli Museum, Tokyo National Museum

Between the two of us we have all of Hayao Miyazaki's movies and would LOVE to see the Ghibli museum.

Sakado

Highlights: Seitenkyu, Meiji Jingu

Seitenkyu is the largest Taoist temple in Japan (Michelle's pick) and Meiji Jingu is a Shinto shrine (Trevor's pick). They both have stunning historic architecture.

Yamanashi

Highlights: Hakone open-air museum, Mt. Fuji

The Hakone museum features over a thousand sculptures and works of art, including artwork by Picasso. Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan and the focus of the Thirty-Six Views of Mt. Fuji, a series of prints by Hokusai.

Nagoya

Highlight: Asahi Brewery Tour

Trevor is a beer lover and we can't miss the opportunity to tour the Asahi Brewery in Nagoya. As time permits we'd also love to see Nagoya Castle and Higashiyama Zoo and Botanical Gardens. Michelle loves botanical gardens!

Kyoto

Highlight: Kinkaku-ji

Kinkaku-ji is a Zen Buddhist temple and one of the most popular places to visit in Japan. It has gorgeous architecture and is set in a strolling garden. If we get time we'd also love to visit the Arashiyama bamboo grove.

Osaka

Highlight: Tempozan Ferris Wheel

No honeymoon is complete without the typical anime Ferris Wheel date. Michelle might be too afraid to ride it but it is impressive to see, at 369 feet tall. It is located at Tempozan harbor next to Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan, which is one of the largest public aquariums in the world. If we get time, we'd also like to see Osaka Castle and the Osaka National Museum of Art.

Himeji

Highlight: Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle is one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites. It is the largest castle in Japan and is a breathtaking example of Japanese architecture.
